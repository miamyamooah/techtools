M3 : Exploring the Wild: How Seek by iNaturalist is Bringing Augmented Reality to Nature Education

What Makes a Video Interactive?

An interactive video incorporates elements that require viewers to engage with the content rather than just watch. Common interactive features include:

  • Quizzes that assess comprehension and reinforce learning.
  • Branching Scenarios that allow learners to make choices and see different outcomes.
  • Clickable Elements that reveal additional information or guide exploration.
  • Simulations that create real-world applications of knowledge.

Example: Ted-Ed’s Interactive Lessons

Context and Audience:

Ted-Ed, an educational platform, uses interactive videos for students and teachers across a wide range of subjects. Their lessons target middle and high school students, providing engaging ways to explore complex concepts.

Interactive Elements:

  • Embedded Questions: Videos pause at key moments to ask questions, prompting learners to think critically.
  • Discussion Prompts: Students can engage with peers by responding to open-ended questions.
  • Additional Resources: Clickable links lead to deeper exploration on the topic.

Effectiveness:

These interactive elements ensure that students do more than passively consume information. Pausing for questions keeps students engaged, and discussion prompts promote reflection. However, the effectiveness can vary—some learners might skip discussions, and not all students engage deeply with external resources.

Example: Duolingo’s Interactive Video Stories

Context and Audience:

Duolingo’s interactive video stories are designed for language learners at various proficiency levels. These videos help learners develop listening and comprehension skills in an engaging format.

Interactive Elements:

  • Fill-in-the-Blank Exercises: Learners complete sentences based on the video’s dialogue.
  • Choose-Your-Response Scenarios: Viewers make decisions that affect the story’s progression.
  • Instant Feedback: Correct and incorrect responses receive immediate explanations.

Effectiveness:

These features mimic real-life conversations, reinforcing language acquisition. The branching dialogue encourages active participation, and instant feedback helps learners correct mistakes immediately. However, some learners might prefer more open-ended speaking opportunities rather than multiple-choice interactions.

Conclusion: Why Interactive Video Works

The best interactive videos succeed because they:

  • Encourage Active Participation – Learners engage by answering questions, making choices, or solving problems.
  • Provide Immediate Feedback – Mistakes become learning moments instead of barriers.
  • Personalize Learning – Branching paths and adaptive elements make content relevant to individual learners.

Interactive video is a powerful tool for education, but its effectiveness depends on thoughtful design. If the interactive elements align with learning goals and the target audience, they can transform passive watching into an engaging, immersive experience.
