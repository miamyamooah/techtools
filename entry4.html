M3 : Exploring the Wild: How Seek by iNaturalist is Bringing Augmented Reality to Nature Education

Imagine this: you’re walking through a park and spot a colorful flower. You pull out your phone, snap a picture, and—voila!—you instantly know that the flower is a Black-eyed Susan. Not only do you learn about the species, but you also discover fun facts about its habitat and environmental significance. All of this is possible with the magic of Augmented Reality (AR), and one of the coolest tools that uses AR in education is the app Seek by iNaturalist.

AR is transforming how we interact with the world around us, making learning immersive, interactive, and, most importantly, fun. In this blog post, we’ll explore how Seek by iNaturalist uses AR to bring nature education to life and why it’s an excellent tool for both students and teachers.

What is Augmented Reality (AR)?

Before we dive into the specifics of Seek, let’s talk about Augmented Reality. AR technology enhances our perception of the real world by overlaying digital information—images, sounds, or other data—on top of our physical environment. Unlike Virtual Reality (VR), which immerses users in a completely virtual environment, AR allows users to interact with both the real world and the virtual world simultaneously.

In education, AR can turn a textbook page into an interactive learning experience, bring historical figures to life, or even allow students to explore distant galaxies from the comfort of their classroom. Whether it’s through smartphones, tablets, or AR glasses, the possibilities of AR in learning are endless!

What is Seek by iNaturalist?

Seek by iNaturalist is an exciting and easy-to-use app that turns your smartphone into a powerful tool for discovering and identifying wildlife. Whether you're in a backyard, on a hike, or even walking around your neighborhood, Seek allows you to snap pictures of plants, animals, and fungi, and instantly identify them.

Think of it as a digital field guide that fits in your pocket—complete with detailed information about each species, including their habitat, characteristics, and role in the ecosystem. What makes Seek truly special, though, is its integration of Augmented Reality (AR). As you point your phone at a plant or animal, the app not only matches your photo to its database but also provides instant feedback, displaying the species name and other educational details right on the screen.

The app also encourages learning through gamification—as users make discoveries, they can earn badges and unlock new achievements, making the experience feel like a nature-based adventure. This interactive and engaging approach helps students and nature enthusiasts alike learn while having fun in the process.

Taking the Classroom Outside with Seek

Outdoor classrooms are more than just a trendy buzzword. With Seek, teachers can easily incorporate nature walks, field trips, or backyard explorations into their lesson plans. Imagine this: Instead of simply reading about ecosystems in textbooks, students are outside identifying trees, flowers, and insects as they encounter them. Seek makes biology lessons come alive!

Here are a few ideas on how Seek can be integrated into your teaching:

  • Nature Walks & Field Trips: Whether you’re at a park, nature reserve, or even your school playground, students can use Seek to identify species they encounter. As they snap photos of plants, animals, or insects, they’ll receive instant feedback on what they’re looking at. You’ll be amazed at how quickly students get excited to find new creatures and learn about them on the spot.
  • STEM Projects: Encourage your students to take on hands-on projects by using Seek to document the species they find over a semester. Students could track the growth of certain plants, monitor seasonal changes in animal behavior, or even document the health of their local ecosystem. The possibilities are endless for building interdisciplinary projects that combine biology, technology, and environmental studies.
  • Create a Nature Journal: Let your students build their own nature journals. Each time they identify a new species, they can record their findings, including a photo of their discovery, fun facts, and any interesting observations. This could become an ongoing class project where students add to their journal throughout the year, turning it into a collection of their discoveries.
  • Classroom Identification Challenges: Turn identification into a classroom game! Have a challenge where students compete to find and identify the most species using Seek during a field trip or on a class hike. Whoever finds the rarest plant or animal wins a prize—how fun is that?

Why AR Makes Seek So Special

So, what makes Seek by iNaturalist stand out? It’s all about the Augmented Reality experience! Instead of simply reading about plants and animals, students can actually interact with the environment around them in a whole new way. The app uses AR to project images of the identified species right onto the screen, allowing students to zoom in, rotate, and learn more about their discovery with just a few taps.

But it’s not just a cool tech trick—it’s about making learning immersive. When students are able to visualize and interact with the very thing they’re studying, it cements the information in their minds far better than traditional methods. Plus, it turns learning into an adventure—one that’s both educational and fun!

Students Love It: The Perfect Blend of Learning and Play

Teachers and students alike are singing Seek’s praises. Research has shown that when students can engage in interactive learning through technology, they’re more motivated to participate and retain information. The game-like features of Seek, where students can “unlock” new species or achievements, help foster that sense of excitement and curiosity.

One of the best parts? It’s all free! Students don’t need expensive equipment or fancy gadgets—just a smartphone or tablet and the Seek app, which they can use anywhere, anytime.

Seek Is the Future of Nature Education

Seek by iNaturalist is more than just an app—it’s a gateway to interactive, immersive learning that takes students out of the classroom and into the wild. With its combination of AR technology, real-time identification, and hands-on exploration, Seek empowers students to engage with the world around them and become active learners.

If you’re looking to make your lessons more exciting, meaningful, and educational, give Seek a try in your next biology class or outdoor lesson. It’s the perfect tool to help students see nature through a whole new lens!
