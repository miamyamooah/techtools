M1 : The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Education

Welcome to my first blog post! Today, we are going to be discussing Artificial Intelligence and how it has rapidly evolved into a daily tool that is used across various industries.

What is AI?

According to the University of Illinois Chicago, AI is as a piece of machine learning technology which is capable of replacing human intellect. This includes, problem solving, create content, detect fraudulence, make decisions, and much more. The earliest records of AI use date back to the 1960's, but did not hold a prominent role in educational technology until the 1990's. In modern society, AI has taken the world by storm and has entered mainstream education, providing teachers with data-driven insights; as well as automating administrative tasks such as grading. This allows educators to spend less time on paperwork and more time on teaching, which benefits students as well.

How Do We Use AI?

Today, AI is used in a variety of ways, including:

  • Personalized Learning: Adjust the difficulty level of specific tasks based on students' individual progress, ensuring no child falls behind while also challenging advanced learners. Applications include DreamBox and Smart Sparrow.
  • Intelligent Tutoring Systems: Provide real-time feedback and guidance. Applications include Squirrel AI.
  • Automated Grading and Administrative Tasks: Help educators automate grading, which in turn frees up time to ensure more impactful time with students. Applications include Gradescope.
  • Ai for Teacher Support: AI-driven insights and lesson planning to better understand specific student needs as well as create customized learning plans. Applications include ChatGPT and Google Classroom AI.
